Business Model Canvas

Key Partners

  • Barangay Council (Legislation & Adoption)
  • Local Philippine National Police (PNP) for formal referencing.
  • Lupong Tagapamayapa (Mediation & Dispute Resolution)
  • Local IT/Tech Volunteers (Initial Support & Maintenance)

Key Activities

  • Software Development and Maintenance.
  • User Training (Security & Analytics).
  • Data Encryption & PDPA Compliance (Philippines Data Privacy Act of 2012).
  • Continuous Content Updates (Local Ordinances).

Value Proposition

Secure, Transparent, and Efficient Incident Reporting.

  • 24/7 instant reporting access (Mobile-friendly).
  • Automated report generation (monthly/annual submissions).
  • Integration of AI-driven insights to identify case trends and high-risk areas.
  • Empowers officials with reliable data for proactive governance.

Customer Relationships

  • Real-time status updates via system notifications (SMS/email).
  • Personalized case management dashboards.
  • Conversational chatbot interface for submissions and updates.
  • Formal Orientation Programs at the Barangay Hall.

Customer Segments

  • Barangay Officials and Staff (Managers/Processors).
  • Lupong Tagapamayapa (Mediators/Resolvers).
  • All Barangay Residents (Complainants, respondents, witnesses).

Key Resources

  • Web/Mobile Platform (Software Asset)
  • Secure Database and Server Infrastructure.
  • Skilled Developers and Maintenance Personnel.
  • Legal/Compliance Expertise (PDPA, Ordinances).

Channels

  • Official Barangay Website/URL.
  • Mobile Application Stores (if deployed natively).
  • Information dissemination via Barangay health centers.
  • Barangay Social Media Pages.

Cost Structure

  • Initial System Development and Design.
  • Server Hosting and Maintenance (Data persistence/security).
  • Continuous Data Security Measures and Audits (Compliance).
  • Training Materials and Labor for Staff Onboarding.

Funding Model (Revenue Streams)

  • Mandated Annual Barangay Budget Allocation (Public safety/digitalization).
  • Potential Grants from Local Government Units (LGUs) for e-governance.
  • Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) funding from private sector partners.
